ü•ó The Core Debate: Sustainability & UPFs

1. Integrating Sustainability into DGAs

The Argument for "Yes" (Feasibility):

  • Efficiency is Up: U.S. agriculture is producing more food with fewer inputs.
  • Resource Management: Corn yields have hit 170+ bushels/acre. Water withdrawal has decreased despite higher output.
  • Tech Enablement: 70% of large farms now use GPS/Precision Ag, allowing for "sustainable intensification."

The Risk (Barriers):

  • Crop Mix: The system is optimized for corn/soy/wheat. Shifting to labor-intensive crops (fruits/veg) requires massive strategy shifts.

2. Addressing Ultra-Processed Foods (UPFs)

The Context:

  • Caloric Density: U.S. produces 2,500+ kcal/person/day.
  • Economic Reality: Corn, Soy, Sugar Beets are the most economically efficient crops to grow due to mechanization.

The Decision Point: Restricting UPFs attacks the economic base of the Corn Belt. Can we pivot to quality over quantity?

üìù The Final Deliverable Strategy

Step 1: The Recommendation

Keep it one sentence. Focus on incorporating sustainability metrics and phased reduction goals.

Step 2: The Rationale

Lead with data. "U.S. producers have achieved historical highs in resource efficiency..."

Step 3: Barriers & Challenges

Address data gaps, school lunch costs, and labor shortages in specialty crops.
